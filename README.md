# On-Off Player Shot Charts
## Background
As someone who is a fan and analyzes the game of basketball, something I've always been curious about is to what extent do players affect how their team's play offense. In a way, its a chicken or the egg question. Do coaches gameplan around their rosters or do general managers build their rosters around their coaches gameplan. While obviously different players carry more or less weight in those discussions, it is a fun thought experiment.
## Data
The data for this project was taken from two APIs. The first was the "nba on court" API which I used to download the play by play and the shot detail data. I was also able to use a function from this package to derive who was on the court during a given play which made this project possible. Second was the NBA_API package, which allowed me to gather the nba player ids, team ids, nba games participated in. These all allowed me to filter the data to individual players.
## Methology
Due to limitations in data, I will be determining team's gameplan/style based on their shot locations. To estimate the effect that a player has on it, I will generate 2 main shot charts. One filtered by the player being on the court and the other with the player off. ![Wemby off court](<Useful files/wemby_off.png>) ![Wemby on court](<Useful files/wemby_on.png>) 
The shot charts are binned by shot location for easier interpretation and they were also placed under a minimum shot filter. This shot filter made it to where the hexes would not appear unless it made up 0.25% of the shots taken. This decision was made to better highlight where the shots were being taken.
One last chart that I created which can add more context to the afformentioned shot charts was my player involvment chart. This chart is a dot plot that shows all the shots made by a team while a player is on the court. I thought it would be interesting to see a player's spots on court where they mostly score and the others where they create shots for others. I also kept the shots made by teammates to show how much of a role they have in the offense. If you see only green then that player more than likely doesn't contribute the offense. ![Wemby Involvement Chart](<Useful files/wemby_involvement.png>)

## Takeaways
My main takeaway from this project is there wasn't as much of an effect as I thought there would be. Granted I have only been able to do individual player analysis due to lack of computing power and having to make multiple api calls for a single player. While there weren't many drastic changes, you can still take insights from from the charts. Specifically with the hex shot charts, you can notice differences between the on and off. Looking back at the Victor Wembanyama examples from earlier, you can see that Spurs get nearly all their offense from the rim and the three point line. While you can brush this off as a league wide trend, the chart with him off the court shows that his teammates take a lot more shots from long floater range. One could make the assumption that Wembanyama being on the court causes his team to take more shots at the rim and get cleaner looks from three. This can be due to his individual scoring at the rim as well as the gravity he has a roll man.

## Final Thoughts
The goal of this project isn't to replace to video and on court scouting but to help shrink a forrest into a couple of trees. Giving scouts ideas to focus on can help them become more efficient and hit on more parts of an opponent. This efficiency is important due to how quick the turn around is game to game. An application of this product could be looking at these shot chart differences in significant players. If you notice a stark difference, you can notate it as something to closely pay attention to during film study and see why that difference takes place. From there, you can bring these findings to coaches to scheme counters. Some weaknesses/room for improvement is the inefficiency of the product. The on court function of the product runs into timeout issues for certain games causing those games to be missing from the sample. This can also cause looking to a single to take multiple minutes generating the shot charts. At scale this adds up and makes the experience very slow. Ways that the product could be improved could be infusing synergy play type data and having all of the data in local database. Calling the data from a database instead of making multiple api calls would lower the excution time and make it easier to turn this product into a usable app. With the synergy play types, adding that to the shot location data would give me a better idea of how the team's offensive process changes
